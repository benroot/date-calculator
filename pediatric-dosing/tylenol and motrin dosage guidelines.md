# Tylenol (Acetaminophen) — Pediatric Dosing

**Weight-Based Dosing**

The recommended oral dose of acetaminophen for pediatric patients is **10–15 mg/kg per dose**, given every **4–6 hours** as needed.[1] The maximum single dose is 15 mg/kg (up to 750 mg), and the maximum daily dose is **75 mg/kg/day** (up to 3,750 mg) for children aged 2–12 years.[2][3] For infants 29 days to 2 years, the maximum daily dose is 60 mg/kg/day; for neonates (≥32 weeks gestational age, up to 28 days), it is 50 mg/kg/day.[2] Acetaminophen is the only recommended analgesic for children under 3 months of age.[1]

**Dosing Frequency:** Every 4–6 hours as needed; do not exceed 5 doses in 24 hours.

**Common Liquid Formulations:**

- **Infant drops/suspension:** 160 mg per 5 mL (this is now the standard concentration for both infant and children's products in the US)[4]
- Older infant concentrated drops (80 mg/0.8 mL) have been discontinued in the US to reduce dosing errors

# Motrin (Ibuprofen) — Pediatric Dosing

**Weight-Based Dosing**

The recommended oral dose of ibuprofen for pediatric patients is **5–10 mg/kg per dose**, given every **6–8 hours** as needed.[6][7] The maximum daily dose is **40 mg/kg/day** (up to 1,200 mg) in the United States.[8] Ibuprofen is **not recommended for infants under 6 months of age** in the US due to risk of acute kidney injury, particularly in the setting of dehydration.[8][9] The recommendation is to use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration.[6]

**Dosing Frequency:** Every 6–8 hours as needed; do not exceed 4 doses in 24 hours.[10][11]

**Common Liquid Formulations:**

- **Infant concentrated drops:** 50 mg per 1.25 mL (i.e., 40 mg/mL)[9]
- **Children's suspension:** 100 mg per 5 mL (i.e., 20 mg/mL) — this is the standard children's liquid[11][4]

**Key Safety Reminders:**

- Acetaminophen overdose can cause **hepatotoxicity**; ensure all sources of acetaminophen (including combination products) are accounted for when calculating total daily dose.[1]
- Ibuprofen carries risks of **renal impairment and GI adverse effects**, and should be used with caution in dehydrated children.[7][8]
- When alternating acetaminophen and ibuprofen, careful attention to dosing intervals is essential to avoid inadvertent overdose.
