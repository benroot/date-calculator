Pediatric Dosing

Weight-based dosing reference for common pediatric medications. Currently covers acetaminophen (Tylenol).

Acetaminophen (Tylenol) Dosing Guidelines

The recommended oral dose is 10–15 mg/kg per dose, given every 4–6 hours as needed. Do not exceed 5 doses in 24 hours.

Maximum single dose is 15 mg/kg, up to 750 mg. Maximum total daily dose depends on age:

  • Neonates (≥32 weeks gestational age, up to 28 days old): 50 mg/kg/day
  • Infants 29 days–2 years: 60 mg/kg/day
  • Children 2–12 years: 75 mg/kg/day, up to 3,750 mg/day

Acetaminophen is the only recommended analgesic for children under 3 months of age.

The standard liquid concentration is 160 mg per 5 mL — this is now the single concentration used for both infant and children's acetaminophen products in the US.

Safety reminder: acetaminophen overdose can cause liver damage. Account for all sources of acetaminophen, including combination products, when calculating total daily dose.
